STEP 1

Send your lists to FeedYu

In Google Maps, open any saved list → Share → pick FeedYu in the share sheet. That's it — the app imports every place the next time you open it.

Friends can share their lists with you the same way — keep up to 20 lists, and switch any of them on or off in Settings without deleting anything.

Your ⭐ Starred places can't be shared by Google — import those once via Google Takeout in Settings.

STEP 2

Get tonight's pick

The Tonight tab suggests one place at a time from your lists — never the same place twice until you've seen everything in range.

Tap the photo to jump straight to the restaurant's own page in Google Maps — reviews, hours, live traffic. Not feeling it? Tap for another.

Long-press a card to hide places you never want suggested again.

STEP 3

Set how far you'll go

The panel at the top of every page is your travel budget. Three ways to measure "close enough":

Distance — a straight-line radius, 200 m to 50 km.
Walk — real walking time, 5 to 60 minutes.
Drive — driving time in current traffic, 15 to 90 minutes.

Slide for big jumps, tap + / − for one step. Each mode remembers its own setting, and changing it re-rolls the suggestion immediately.

STEP 4

Feeling fancy? Ask the Michelin tab

The Michelin tab plays the same game over the entire Michelin Guide — every tier, worldwide, built in. No setup needed.

Filter by price band and award (Selected, Bib Gourmand, one to three stars), then let it pick. It also lists everything in range, nearest first — every row taps through to Google Maps — including places that dropped off the guide, if you ask.

Names show in the local language — 鮨さいとう, not "Sushi Saito".

STEP 5

Staying in? Same lists, delivered

The Uber Eats tab suggests from the same saved places — but only ones it has verified you can actually order from, matched to the exact branch by location.

The photo still opens Google Maps for reviews. The green button drops you inside the store on Uber Eats, ready to order — not a search page.

Shops, closed places, and restaurants not on the platform are skipped automatically.

STEP 6

Swipe your way around

Swipe left or right anywhere to move between the four pages — or tap the bar at the bottom.

In Settings you manage everything: switch lists on and off (great for testing a friend's list for a week), rename them, remove them safely — a place that's also on another list is never deleted — and re-sync anytime. Lists refresh themselves weekly.

Good to know

Everything stays on your phone

Your lists and places live in a local store. No account, no cloud, no tracking — location is only used to measure "how far is dinner".

Traffic is checked, not guessed

Walk and drive budgets verify the real route with Apple Maps at suggestion time — "30 min drive" means 30 minutes right now, not on an empty road.

Hidden means hidden

Long-press any suggestion to hide it forever. Hidden places survive re-syncs; unhide them anytime in Settings → Manage restaurants.
